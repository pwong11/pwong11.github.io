About SmartDart

It can be challenging to learn how to play darts and throw them accurately. Our adaptive dartboard gradually adapts over time in difficulty.

SmartDart detects how well the player is throwing darts and adjusts the difficulty by changing the target size and distance.

Our features

Sensors

Piezoelectric sensors are attached to the back of each dartboard to detect hits. After each round of darts, the user presses an arcade button to indicate that he/she has finished.

Actuators

Stepper motors are used to move the dartboards horizontally through rack and pinion mechanisms. A stepper motor is also used to adjust the target distance using a timing belt and idler pulley system.

User Interface

An LCD screen provides instructions and feedback to the user.

MILESTONES

Milestone 1

Our first prototype of a dartboard. A piezosensor attached to the back of the dartboard detects when a dart hits the board.

MILESTONES

Milestone 2

A demonstration of working actuation methods. The stepper motor is connected to a driver and its movements are controlled by an Arduino.

MILESTONES

Milestone 3

Prototypes of all three dartboards constructed. The dartboard diameters are 12", 8", and 4".

MILESTONES

Milestone 4

Timing belt and idler pulley system constructed.

MILESTONES

Milestone 5

SmartDart constructed. Stepper motors move the dartboards using rack and pinion mechanisms. The dartboards sit on a base that is attached to the timing belt and idler pulley system that adjusts target distance. There is an additional layer supporting everything underneath for extra height.
